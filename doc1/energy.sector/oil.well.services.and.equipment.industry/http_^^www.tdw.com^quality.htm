Job Description: SHIPFITTER

General Description:

Lays out and fabricates metal structural parts, such as plates, bulkheads, and frames, and braces them in position within hull of ship for welding. Lays out position of parts on metal, working from blueprints or templates and using scribe and handtools. Locates and marks reference lines, such as center, buttock, and frame lines. Aligns parts in relation to each other, using jacks, turnbuckles, clips, wedges and mauls. Marks location of holes to be drilled and installs temporary fasteners to hold part in place for welding. Installs packing, gaskets, liners and structural accessories and members, such as doors, hatches, brackets and clips. May tack weld clips and brackets in place prior to permanent welding. Rigs chainfalls.

Essential Functions:

The individual assigned to this position must, with or without reasonable accommodation, be able to perform the following Essential Functions:

  • Comprehend blueprints, sketches and templates
  • Enter and move through manholes to reach and work in internally confined spaces
  • Manipulate and transport loads of up to 50 pounds over distances of up to 200 feet, approximately 1 to 6 times during each 8 hour shift
  • Manipulate and operate and machines and handpower tools essential to the performance of the position of Shipfitter
  • Traverse gangways and scaffolding at varying heights
  • Traverse and negotiate uneven heights between dock and vessel
  • Traverse and negotiate uneven surfaces
  • Ascend and work at heights of up to 30 feet
  • Stooping, squatting, twisting, pulling and pushing

 

 

Job Description: PIPEFITTER

General Description:

Unless otherwise defined by applicable law, any employment relationship with this organization is of an "at will" nature, which means that the Employee may resign at any time and the Employer may discharge Employee at any time with or without cause.

Performs layout, fabrication, installation, testing and repair of pipe systems. Fits pipe and properly prepares joints for welding to meet the specific requirements on the system on which work is being done. Works from blueprints and makes fabrication sketches from which another mechanic could fabricate another unit of pipe. Lays out and figures offset bends and rolling offset bends.
