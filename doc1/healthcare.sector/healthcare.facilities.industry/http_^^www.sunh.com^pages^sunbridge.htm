What is assisted living?
The term is used to describe a wide range of living arrangements for older people. Generally, assisted living offers housing, healthcare and support services for people who are no longer able to live on their own, but who can maintain a higher degree of independence than most residents in skilled nursing facilities. Often, family members or friends participate in residents' care. Some assisted living facilities offer mainly help with the basic activities of daily living, such as dressing, eating, hygiene and grooming, mobility and housekeeping; others emphasize healthcare services and have a more institutional environment.

What will SunBridge residences be like?
SunBridge Assisted Living Residences will set itself apart in the industry by placing equal emphasis on three components – upscale housing, hotel-level service and superior healthcare. Each facility will have more than 90 private apartments, either studios or one-bedroom residences. We are even able to arrange for two-bedroom apartments in some of our residences. Each apartment will have a kitchenette, although three meals a day will be served in our central dining area. The emphasis will be on quality, value and service.

Why is assisted living such a rapidly growing business?
The U.S. Census Bureau projects that in the 1990s, the population group in the 75- to 84- year-old range will grow by 21 percent, and the population over 85 will grow by 42 percent. As more people live longer and remain healthier, they need different options for living arrangements. Family members are not always able to be full-time caregivers, particularly in households where both adults work outside the home. Long-term care facilities, meanwhile, are providing increasingly higher levels of skilled nursing care. Many older people are too frail to live alone, but can have a high quality of life in a more independent, residential setting with some key support services. A typical assisted living resident is female, in her 80s, and widowed or single.

Where will SunBridge build its residences?
Because this is a private-pay arrangement – not covered by Medicare or insurance – SunBridge is targeting major metropolitan areas with a large upper-income population. The company will market its services to three groups: older people themselves; their adult children, who often play a key role in the decision to choose assisted living; and referral sources, such as physicians, social workers and hospital discharge planners. The top potential markets include Chicago, Los Angeles, Kansas City, Cleveland, St. Louis, Dallas and Houston. SunBridge opened its first residence in Denver in August, and will have facilities in Tucson and Atlanta by the end of 1997.
