    News Release: Index of Investor Optimism
    New York, October 16, 1997 - Optimism among American investors increased significantly during the last three months, indicating greater confidence in the Federal government's ability to achieve fiscal reform and in the continued steady growth of the economy, according to the fourth PaineWebber/Gallup Index of Investor Optimism. Overall, the latest index findings, released today as the 10th anniversary of the 1987 market correction approaches, show that investor optimism is at its highest level since the index was established a year ago. The index also shows that optimism among female investors has improved considerably, though a gender gap remains and has nearly tripled since it was first identified in October 1996.
