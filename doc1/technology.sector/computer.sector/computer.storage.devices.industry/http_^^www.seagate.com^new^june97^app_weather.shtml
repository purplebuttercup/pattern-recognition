Seagate Drive
Aids in Weather
Watching

 

With a little assistance from Seagate's new Medalist Pro 6450 drive, WRIC-TV meteorologist John Bernier helps residents of the mid Atlantic region stay one step ahead of potentially dangerous weather conditions.

Bernier, who has worked for the ABC affiliate in Richmond, Virginia, since 1984, added Seagate's 6.4 Gbyte drive into his system at home. The result: he can download current radar images directly from the TV station's weather network and monitor weather conditions at all times.

"The expanded storage capacity allows me to look at archived data," who also uses an IBM 3.2 Gbyte DeskStar drive at home. "I can now view several radar images at one time without zipping them up."

 

Storage Space A Premium

While each image is singularly small, with largest satellites photo taking up about 7 Mbytes of storage space, Bernier may download as many as 60 images at one sitting. "I used to think twice about loading up with a lot of stuff," says the Emmy-award-winning Bernier. "With 6.4 gigabytes of storage space I don't have to think at all."

In addition to his television work, Bernier serves as meteorologist for Agrinet Farm Radio Network in Richmond, Virginia, and radio station WCXL in Kill Devil Hills, North Carolina. Because WCXL is located on the coast, Bernier' must constantly monitor conditions during hurricane season. "Because of the 6450's capabilities, I can get a head start on the day by download radar images onto my system at home," says Bernier, who graduated Magna Cum Laude from University of Lowell. "I start pulling down images at five in the morning."

 

Effective Technology

In addition to its vast storage space, Bernier says the 6450's ATA-2 interface provides the ideal solution for work on a single system. "I don't notice the absence of a SCSI interface," says Bernier, referring to the interface that many equate with optimal performance. "You have to look at how many applications you have open and running at the same time. For home computing with one system, I don't think most people would notice that they don't have a SCSI interface."

 

Partitioning Means Safety

Bernier says the extra storage capacity allows him to partition his drive into large enough segments to provide a practical working environment. "I can partition and password protect my workspace and keep my children out of that area," says Bernier, referring to one of the challenges of working at home. "If my kids run into a problem in their partition, it doesn't effect my mission critical data."

After downloading the images onto his 5,400-RPM drive, Bernier is able to issue weather bulletins on the fly with the 6450's average seek time of 9.5 msec. "The 6450 is sweet," he says. "It's very dependable, and the access time is extremely fast."

 

Time Is Critical

Because of Bernier's commitment, residents of the mid Atlantic region can rest easier during times of inclement weather conditions.

Who would have imagined that an efficient disc drive could help save lives and property? John Bernier. That's why he chose Seagate's high performance Medalist Pro 6450.
