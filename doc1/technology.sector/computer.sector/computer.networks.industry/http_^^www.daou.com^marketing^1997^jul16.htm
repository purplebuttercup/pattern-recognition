DAOU Systems Announces Financial Results for Second Quarter ended June 30, 1997
    SAN DIEGO, July 16, 1997 -- DAOU Systems (NASDAQ:DAOU) Wednesday reported record second-quarter revenue accompanied by increases in net income and earnings per share.

    The company posted second-quarter and year-to-date revenue of $6 million and $11.2 million respectively, increases over the previous year of 15 percent and 49 percent respectively. Net income for the second quarter was $58,000 with a net loss for the year to date of $174,000.

    That translates to second-quarter earnings per share of 1 cent, the same as for the second quarter of the previous year. The year-to-date earnings per share are a loss of 2 cents, which compares with a loss of 1 cent for the same period in 1996.

    The increase in revenue is primarily attributable to an increase in the number of network management and implementation contracts that the company has secured with such major health care organizations as Harris Methodist Health System, Centura Health and St. Mary's Health Network.

    These and other management contracts have boosted the company's recurring revenue to 33 percent for the year to date. The company's net income continues to be impacted by increased expenses associated with the regionalization of the company's sales organization, the addition of sales personnel and the expansion of marketing programs. The company reported total sales and marketing expenses for the second quarter of $1.1 million, an increase of 186 percent over the $376,000 reported for the same period in 1996.

    "We are continuing to roll out our corporate strategy,'' said Georges Daou, chief executive officer of DAOU Systems. "All our planned regional offices have been established within the past 120 days and are productive.

    "In addition, we have established key alliances like the recently announced marketing agreement with VHA, a major health care purchasing group. Such alliances will help provide our company with entry and exposure to new and larger markets. In the case of VHA, DAOU will be the vendor of choice for more than 1,500 of VHA's community health care members.

    "The solidification of our sales and marketing foundation also allows the company to offer a broader line of services that include our new desktop support, which is now being provided to Harris Methodist Health System.''

    DAOU Systems also reported that its full network outsourcing contract with Candler Hospital will not continue past 1997 due to a recent acquisition of Candler Hospital by St. Joseph's hospital. The company expects positive results from this event.

    There will be no reduction in expected revenue for 1997, and the $600,000 contract-cancellation penalty will augment DAOU's net income for the year. The event also frees the company to focus on opportunities in desktop support services which generally yield higher margins and are a good match with DAOU's core competencies.

    The desktop support contract with Harris Methodist Health System is representative of such opportunities and is expected to replace revenue lost from the cancellation of the Candler Hospital contract.

    Founded in 1987, DAOU Systems designs, implements and manages computer networks and desktop operations for the health care industry. The company also offers outsourcing, Internet and network monitoring solutions. DAOU has provided network services to more than 450 hospitals and health care facilities nationwide.

    With headquarters in San Diego, the company has five regional offices and more than 230 employees. DAOU's clients include such organizations as Centura Health, Lutheran Health Systems, Mercy Health Services, Harris Methodist Health System and Catholic Medical Center of Brooklyn and Queens.
