Overview

Interface Systems, Inc. (ISI) is a 30 year old, established, multi-national corporation with annual revenue in excess of $75 million. ISI develops and markets printers, document management, presentation, and connectivity solutions for customers using IBM enterprise networks and open systems. Based in Ann Arbor, Michigan with more than 200 employees worldwide, Interface Systems' products are sold in the US via direct sales, distributors, and value-added resellers. Export sales are managed through our wholly-owned subsidiary, Interface Systems International, Ltd. in London, England. Direct sales offices and distributors can be found throughout the world.

History

Founded in 1967, Interface Systems Inc. began as a software provider for mainframe systems. Since then, the company has grown to become a world-wide supplier of hardware and software for IBM Advanced Function Printing (AFP), UNIX-to-mainframe connectivity, and electronic data interchange (EDI).

In 1975, ISI began manufacturing IBM-compatible products for mainframes and midrange computers. In the early 1980's, the company had developed the first Intelligent Printer Datastream (IPDS) plug-compatible printer. Corporate acquisitions in the mid-1980's and early 1990's lead the company into the data communications industry and increased the firm's international presence.

In 1995, ISI introduced Oasis, a distributed document delivery solution that handles complex mainframe-to-printer communications. Used in conjunction with Microsoft's SNA Server or other popular SNA gateways, Oasis allows networks to deliver output in open system environments without special equipment or training.

Interface Systems, Inc. is dedicated to expanding its resources in the data communications and document delivery industries. To this end, we consistently deliver quality products that meet the fundamental needs of our customers.
