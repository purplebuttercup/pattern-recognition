JOB DESCRIPTION: Manager Organizational Development & Training

Title: Manager, Organizational Development & Training

Reports To: Vice President, Human Resources

Summary: Plans and implements systems and programs pertaining to organizational development, employee learning, performance management, and career planning in order to facilitate increased productivity and organizational effectiveness.

Duties and Responsibilities:

Organizational Development

1. Assists senior management in conducting a periodic organizational review process, to assess manpower performance and needs, succession plans, identifying high potential employees, and to determine ways to strengthen the organization via training and development.

2. Works with business managers and other line managers to plan and implement individual employee development programs. Provides counsel on specific learning and development opportunities.

3. Recommends, as appropriate, strategies and approaches to improve operating effectiveness through changes in organizational structure, staffing, and reporting relationships.

Employee Learning

4. Conducts in-house training programs for supervisory, leadership, interpersonal, and managerial skills utilizing both licensed and custom developed training materials. Recommends utilization of external training resources for in-house training programs where appropriate and cost-justified.

5. Working with senior managers and department heads, assists in identifying appropriate, cost-effective external or internal (PC based software) training programs to address the needs of individual employees at all organizational levels.

Performance Management

6. Through group training and individual coaching, supports the development and enhancement of knowledge and skills for management staff to productively use performance management systems.

7. Recommends improvements to existing performance management systems, as appropriate.

Career Development

8. Assists senior management in creating individual development plans for key and high potential employees, and in regular follow-up activities to assure that such plans are carried out.

Other

9. Recommends strategies and programs to assist in the HR management of change (acquisitions, divestitures, cultural, restructuring).

10. Provides recommendations and insights to HR staff concerning business needs and opportunities where their expertise may be applied.

11. Maintains awareness of state-of-the-art learning technology to be applied in employee skills enhancement.
