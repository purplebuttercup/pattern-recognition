Wort Cooling &
Refrigeration Systems

Mueller Wort Cooling Systems are available in a wide range of capacities. Specifically designed to provide the best wort cooling possible for your needs and taking into account volume, time, and energy requirements, each of the Mueller Wort Cooling systems have been custom fitted with the best cooling option for the application's load.



MaximICE

The Mueller MaximICE Liquid Thermal Energy Storage System is a cost-effective way to use thermal energy storage to satisfy the wort cooling load. It allows you to shift your energy consumption to lower-cost, off-peak hours. Using a mixture of water and glycol, MaximICE produces a non-toxic, pumpable ice slurry that can be stored for use during peak demand. The entire MaximICE system consists of two cooling loops: an ice-making loop and an ice-melting loop. The storage tank is common to both loops as it separates ice manufacturing from ice usage. The tank contains a glycol/water solution which is converted to an ice slurry in the ORE. The slurry melts as the stored ice absorbs the heat of the cooling load. MaximICE is an energy efficient, reliable, and affordable way to satisfy your wort cooling needs.


Accu-Therm PHE

The Mueller Accu-Therm Plate Heat Exchanger is a compact heat exchanger, consisting of embossed stainless steel heat transfer plates, with perimeter gaskets to contain pressure and control the flow of each medium. The gasketed plates are assembled in a pack, mounted on upper and lower guide rails, and compressed between two end frames by compression bolts. The Accu-Therm Plate Heat Exchanger cools with precise temperature control.
