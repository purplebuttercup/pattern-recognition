Company Descriptions

Millennium Chemicals consists of Millennium Petrochemicals Inc., Millennium Inorganic Chemicals Inc.,Millennium Specialty Chemicals Inc. and a 26% ownership in Suburban Propane (NYSE - SPH), the third largest U.S. distributor of propane gas.

MILLENNIUM PETROCHEMICALS is the largest U.S. manufacturer of polyethylene and the largest producer of industrial ethyl alcohol, diethyl ether and polyolefin powders in the U.S. It is also the second largest U.S. producer of vinyl acetate monomer, acetic acid, color concentrates for plastics, specialty polymers for adhesives, sealants and coatings, wire and cable compounds and tie-layer for multi-layer packaging. It is also a major producer of polyproplene and methanol. In 1996, Millennium Petrochemicals used or sold approximately 4.5 billion pounds of ethylene, of which approximately 3.7 billion pounds were produced at Millennium Petrochemicals' three ethylene production facilities. Millennium Petrochemicals is headquartered in Cincinnati, OH with polyethylene plants in Morris, IL, La Porte, TX, Port Arthur, TX and Clinton, IA.
MILLENNIUM INORGANIC CHEMICALS is the third largest producer of titanium dioxide ("TiO2") in the world, the second largest producer of TiO2 in the U.S. It is also the world's second largest producer of TiO2 by the environmentally preferred chloride production process. This principal business accounts for approximately 96% of net sales, which is principally sold to the coatings, paper, plastics and elastomer industries. Millennium Inorganic Chemicals sold approximately 450,000 metric tons of TiO2 pigments in fiscal 1996. Millennium Inorganic Chemicals is headquartered in Baltimore, MD with plants located in Baltimore, MD; Ashtabula, OH; Stallingborough, England and Bunbury, Australia.
MILLENNIUM SPECIALTY CHEMICALS is one of the leading producers of terpene based fragrance ingredients derived from crude sulfate turpentine, a by-product of the kraft process of papermaking. Millennium Specialty Chemicals sells over 250 specialty organic chemicals based principally on terpene chemistry, which are used for fragrance, flavor, pharmaceutical and industrial applications. Approximately 80% of total sales are for aroma chemical use. About 60% of sales are export and are made by Millennium Specialty Chemicals sales force directly to manufacturers and through distributors and agents. Millennium Specialty Chemicals is headquartered in Jacksonville, FL and has plants in Brunswick, GA and Jacksonville, FL.

Meet the Millennium Management Team

Upon the completion of the spin-off of Millennium Chemicals on October 1, the new management team consists of the following :

William M. Landuyt- Chairman & CEO. Bill joined Hanson Industries in June 1983 as Assistant Treasurer and became Controller in December 1983, VP of Finance in 1987 and VP and CFO in 1988. He was appointed Associate Director of Hanson PLC in 1990 and joined the Board of Hanson PLC as Finance Director in 1992. Bill was appointed President and Chief Executive Officer of Hanson Industries in June 1995.
John E. Lushefski- Sr. Vice President & CFO. Jack joined Hanson Industries in 1985 as Group Controller. In 1987, he became Corporate Controller and in 1990 was made VP and Controller. He left the Corporate office in August 1991 to become the VP and CFO of Peabody Holding Company. He returned in 1995 at his present title.
George H. Hempstead, III- Sr. VP Law and Administration, George first joined Hanson in 1976, left in 1978 and returned in 1982 as VP and General Counsel. In 1986 he was named a Director of Hanson Industries and in 1990 an Associate Director of Hanson PLC. George was named Sr. VP and General Counsel in December 1993. In June 1995, George was appointed to the position of Sr. VP Law and Administration.
Robert E. Lee - President and Chief Executive Officer Millennium Inorganic Chemicals. Bob joined Hanson Industries in 1982 and held various positions in the financial area before becoming Treasurer in 1987. He was appointed to Vice President and Treasurer in 1990, and assumed the position of Vice President and Chief Financial Officer in 1992. In 1995, he was appointed Senior Vice President and Chief Operating Officer of Hanson Industries, and became President and Chief Operating Officer of Millennium Chemicals when it was demerged from Hanson in October 1996. Bob is a Director Of Millennium chemicals and Vice Chairman of its Operations Committee.
George W. Robbins- President and Chief Executive Officer Millennium Specialty Chemicals. George has been associated with the plastics or chemicals industries for almost 30 years. He joined Millennium Specialty Chemical in 1982 as VP and General Manager of SCM Organic Chemicals Division in Jacksonville, FL. After Hanson acquired SCM in 1986, he became Chairman, President and CEO of the company. George holds a BA from Virginia Military Institute.
Ronald H. Yocum- President and Chief Executive Officer Millennium Petrochemicals Company. Dr. Yocum joined Millennium Petrochemicals in 1987 as Group Vice President, Research and Development. Prior to 1987 he held the same position for Enron Chemical which was acquired by Millennium Petrochemicals. In 1989, Ron was named President and CEO of Millennium Petrochemicals Company and in 1995 he was also appointed Chairman. He received his undergraduate degree in chemistry from Gettysburg College and his Ph.D. in organic chemistry from the University of Pennsylvania.
