National Fuel Gas Supply Corporation transports and stores natural gas for National Fuel's local distribution area, as well as for utilities, pipelines and other companies in the growing markets of the northeastern United States. Situated at the "crossroads" of the pipeline network serving the northeast, this subsidiary owns and operates a 3,241 mile pipeline network that extends from southwestern Pennsylvania to the Canadian gateway at Niagara Falls. It also owns and operates 30 underground natural gas storage areas and is co-owner and operator of four others. Most of its operations are regulated by the Federal Energy Regulatory Commission.
