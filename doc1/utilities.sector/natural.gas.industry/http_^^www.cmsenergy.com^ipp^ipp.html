CMS Generation provides project development, engineering, management, and operations
 for independent power plants in the U.S., Latin America, Africa, South Asia, and the Pacific Rim. 
As a key operating unit of CMS Energy, we offer unique expertise in all the skills required 
to build and manage complex power projects, particularly in developing countries, 
where energy demand is growing 5-10 percent annually.

































CMS Generation has interests in 33 operating plants totalling 6,600 
gross megawatts (MW). Our net generating capacity has more than doubled since 1991. Projects range in size from 3 megawatts to 2,000 megawatts. Fuel types
include hydro, coal, natural gas, oil, wood, wind, and waste material. 




In 1990, CMS Generation became the first organization in the world to 
successfully
convert an abandoned nuclear facility to a natural gas-fueled, 
combined-cycle
plant: the Midland Cogeneration Venture. This project, the largest 
cogeneration
plant in the U.S., has attracted major comment and analysis in the 
international
power-generation community.
