Dominguez Water was formed in 1911 to irrigate the farmlands of the Rancho San Pedro, the first Spanish Land Grant in the South Bay area of Los Angeles County. Back then, Dominguez provided a few thousand gallons per day with one artesian well, steam-engine powered pumps, and redwood pipes.

Today, Long Beach-based Dominguez Water Company is the largest subsidiary of Dominguez Services Corporation. The utility provides up to 50 million gallons per day to business and residential customers, utilizing local groundwater and water imported by the Metropolitan Water District of Southern California from the State Water Project and the Colorado River. The company's professional staff of engineers, operators, and service providers support the operations of smaller subsidiaries in Kern County and northern Los Angeles County.

Dominguez Services Corporation continues to build on its tradition of excellence, utilizing the latest technologies and the talents of its employees to achieve growth in the water industry. It is a water resource management company specializing in the delivery of a reliable, high-quality water supply at the lowest possible cost. Its mission is to improve the quality of life for those it serves by:

  • Exceeding customer expectations
  • Providing investors a competitive rate of return
  • Creating a productive and fulfilling environment for employees
  • Contributing to the well-being of its communities

  
Dominguez is guided by its commitment to continuous improvement, fiscal responsibility, technological advancement, business growth, customer satisfaction, and employee development.
