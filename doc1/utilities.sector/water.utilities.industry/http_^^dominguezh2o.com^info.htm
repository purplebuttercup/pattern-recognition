May Is National Water Awareness Month
You use water every day, from the time you wake up in the morning to the time you go to sleep at night. So why is it necessary to recognize May as National Water Awareness Month? Because most people don’t think about their water unless they’re being plagued by flood or drought. Take a moment this month to consider how important water is to all of us. We need it to drink, bathe, grow our food, and keep our businesses going. Indeed, water is our livelihood, and we need to do all we can to conserve and protect it -- not just in May, but in every month of the year.

Save Water, Save Money
More water is used in the bathroom than in any other place in the home. Install ultra low-flush toilets, low-flow showerheads, and faucet aerators, and you’ll save thousands of gallons of water a year.

Did you know?
There is the same amount of water on earth today as there was when the earth was formed. In fact, the water from your faucet could contain molecules that dinosaurs drank. But of all the water on earth, 97% is salty or otherwise undrinkable; 2% is locked in ice caps and glaciers; and only 1% is available for our agricultural, residential, manufacturing, community, and personal needs.
